Rope Rescue

Rescue Mission

Competencies

grip strengthconfidencebilateral integration

EYFS Criteria

[PD] Climbing apparatus[PD] Bilateral coordination[PSED] Independence & choice-making[PSED] Self-regulation

Key Movements

rope grid ascentrope to ladder lateral transferladder climb to topmonkey bars traverseclimbing wall descent

Route

Rope grid base›Rope grid mid›Ladder mid›Top platform (ladder side)›Top platform (rope/wall side)›Climbing wall mid (grips)›Climbing wall base

Setup

  • Bean bag at top platform (rope/wall side) — the stranded animal
  • Position yourself between rope grid mid and the top

Activity

StepsPrompts
1

Start at rope grid base. Point to the bean bag at the top.

"There's an animal stuck up there. How do you think you'll get to it?"
2

Child climbs the rope grid.

"Hold the rope with both hands, one foot at a time."
3

At rope grid mid, child transfers across to ladder mid.

"What does your body need to do first?"
4

Climb ladder to top platform.

"Strong hands, strong feet."
5

Monkey bars across to bean bag at far top platform.

If they drop: "Your hands got tired. Try again or find another way?"
6

Child picks up the bean bag.

"How are you going to carry it and still hold on?"
7

Descend climbing wall grips to climbing wall base.

"Slow feet. Feel the wall."
8

Finish at climbing wall base. Debrief.

"What was the hardest part? What did your hands have to do on the bars?"

Adult Guidance

Model grip once, then step back
Wrap your fingers around, both hands on — then move away. Don't re-model mid-climb.
Silence means planning
If the child is still and looking, they are working it out. Don't fill the pause.
The carry is the challenge
Don't suggest how to hold the bean bag on the way down. The problem-solving is the point.
Let them find another way
If monkey bars feel too hard, offer a choice: go back the way they came or try the wall. The rescue still completes.

Variations

  • 1. Two rescues: second bean bag at rope grid mid — collect on the way up or make a second trip
  • 2. Sleeping creature: don't wake it — child must move slowly and controlled
  • 3. Pair version: one child up the rope grid, one up the ladder — meet at the top, one crosses for the rescue

Free Play

  • ✧ Where should the animal live on the apparatus?
  • ✧ Can you make a route to get it home?
  • ✧ What if there were two to rescue?

Why This Works

The rope grid ascent demands bilateral grip and weight transfer from the first move. The monkey bars are the curriculum peak — both hands working in sequence, grip sustaining load, child self-regulating pace. Carrying the bean bag on descent adds a genuine self-managed constraint: the child must solve a real problem rather than follow a prescribed technique, building confidence through agency rather than instruction.
